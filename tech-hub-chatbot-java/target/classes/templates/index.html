Build Gardens
and defeat Food Insecurity

Many individuals in South Africa lack the basic knowledge and skills to develop and maintain food gardens. This leads to a heavy reliance on buying food, which is becoming increasingly expensive due to economic factors. This project aims to address food insecurity by providing practical, technology-driven education to communities about how to start and maintain small-scale gardens.

Teaching communities how to start and sustain food gardens, thereby improving food security

Food insecurity continues to be a major concern across South Africa, particularly among households with limited income. Although gardening could be a sustainable and cost-effective solution, it is often overlooked due to a lack of knowledge, resources, or support systems. This leads to a heavy reliance on buying food, which is becoming increasingly expensive due to economic factors. As a result, families with limited income often suffer from food insecurity, poor nutrition, and even malnutrition. Gardening is also perceived as time-consuming and complicated, which discourages people from starting their own food production. Major barriers that prevent people from starting their own gardens

  • Lack of knowledge about how to start
  • Limited space and resources
  • Cost of gardening supplies
  • Lack of motivation or support systems
